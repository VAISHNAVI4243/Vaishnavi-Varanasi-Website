UB Research Assistant, an AI-powered tool for the University at Buffalo community to simplify academic research. Its objective was to automate paper summarization, provide real-time recommendations, and match users with relevant UB faculty. Technically, it leverages LLMs, ChromaDB, Streamlit, and APIs like Semantic Scholar and arXiv for semantic search, summarization, and recommendation. The output is a user-friendly platform that reduces research time, enhances collaboration, and provides actionable insights. Challenges included handling abstract-less papers, selecting flexible APIs, and designing an intuitive interface. Through this project, I learned to integrate AI, databases, and UX design to create a functional academic tool.

The UB Research Assistant is an AI-powered academic tool designed specifically for the University at Buffalo community. It simplifies the research process for students, faculty, and scholars by offering quick paper summarization, smart recommendations, and a collaboration matching system with UB faculty. By combining the power of language models, semantic search, and open-access APIs, the tool helps users discover, digest, and connect around cutting-edge academic work.

  • Automate the summarization of research papers using LLMs
  • Provide real-time recommendations of similar papers
  • Enable users to find UB faculty members working on related research areas
  • Reduce time spent on reading and searching through disconnected platforms
  • Lay the groundwork for future global collaboration beyond UB
  • Research Paper Search: via Semantic Scholar and arXiv APIs
  • Paper Summarization: using a locally served language model (Mistral via Ollama)
  • Paper Recommendation Engine: based on keyword relevance and embeddings
  • UB Faculty Research Matcher: matches user keywords with UB faculty research stored in a local JSON DB and ChromaDB
  • Python Version: 3.11
  • Libraries: Streamlit, ChromaDB, Requests, Ollama
  • APIs Used: Semantic Scholar, arXiv
  • UI Framework: Streamlit
  • Selecting the most flexible and open API (Semantic Scholar vs. arXiv)
  • Handling abstract-less papers
  • Designing a clean yet informative UI
  • Piloted with users, improving habit consistency by 20%. The 20% improvement was calculated by comparing the average completion rate of targeted habits before and after using the AI coach. For example, if users completed their habits 50% of the time before the pilot and 60% of the time during/after the pilot, the improvement is 20%.
